Different Types of The Human Body System

Skelatal System

The human skeletal system consists of bones, cartilage, ligaments and tendons and accounts for about 20 percent of the body weight.

Muscular System

The muscular system is composed of specialized cells called muscle fibers. Their predominant function is contractibility.

Nervous System

The nervous system is the major controlling, regulatory, and communicating system in the body.

Endocrine System

The endocrine system, along with the nervous system, functions in the regulation of body activities.

Cardiovascular System

The cardiovascular system is sometimes called the blood-vascular, or simply the circulatory, system.

Lymphatic System

Lymph capillaries pick up the excess interstitial fluid and proteins and return them to the venous blood. After the fluid enters the lymph capillaries, it is called lymph.

Respiratory System

The respiratory system works with the circulatory system to provide this oxygen and to remove the waste products of metabolism.

Digestive System

The digestive system includes the digestive tract and its accessory organs, which process food into molecules that can be absorbed and utilized by the cells of the body.

Urinary System

The principal function of the urinary system is to maintain the volume and composition of body fluids within normal limits.

Reproductive System

The major function of the reproductive system is to ensure survival of the species.
